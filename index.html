Historical Place In India

1. Taj Mahal

Description

The Taj Mahal was commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1631, to be built in the memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died on 17 June that year while giving birth to their 14th child, Gauhara Begum.[10][11] Construction started in 1632, and the mausoleum was completed in 1648, while the surrounding buildings and garden were finished five years later.[12][13]

2. Qutub-minar

Description

Qutub Minar, built by the Mughal Emperor Qutub Uddin Aibak, is believed to be the finest example of Indo-Muslim architecture. You can see the intricate detailing of the storeys made with red sandstone. The detailing are mostly in Nagari and Arabic language, and mostly the verses of the Quran are written on the walls.

3. Red-Fort

Description

The construction of the Red Fort started in the year 1638 and took almost ten years to complete in the year 1648. The fort was constructed when Shah Jahan shifted the capital from Agra to Delhi, and during that time, the fort was known as Qila-e-Mubarak. The Red Fort is one the most famous historical places in India, contributing largely to historical tourism in India, and it is also one of the places where the president of India gives his annual speech on Independence Day.

4. Agra-fort

Description

This historical site has two gates; one is the Amar Singh Gate, and the other is the Delhi Gate. If you want to uncover the ancient city filled with mosques, gateways, courts, and passages, you should enter from the Amar Singh gate. The Agra Fort is one of the most beautiful places to visit in Agra and was used at the time of shooting the popular movie Jodha Akbar, featuring Hrithik Roshan and Aishwarya Rai.

5. Hawa-Mahal

Description

The Hawa Mahal was built by the ruler Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, and the upper part of the monument was shaped like that a crown because the ruler was a devotee of Lord Krishna. Hawa Mahal is said to be the tallest building in the world without a foundation. The whole palace is a little curved but stands still due to its pyramidical shape. As per legends, it is also believed that the reason for making several windows was for the ladies so that they looked outside as they were under constant ‘purdah’.

6. Victoria-memorial

Description

A lush garden surrounds the memorial, and as you enter the museum, you can see that the place is filled with old weapons, ornaments, dresses, and paintings of the era when the britishers ruled India. The best painting that you will come across in the memorial is that of Prince of Wales in 1876 in Jaipur, painted by Russian artist Vasili Verestchagen.

7. Golden-temple

Description

The Golden Temple in Punjab is one of the most popular Gurudwaras in the world. The Harminder Sahib Gurudwara is undoubtedly one of the most spiritual historical places in India, and witnesses hundreds and thousands of pilgrims every year who pay a visit, irrespective of their religious beliefs. The temple was built in 1599 by Guru Ram Das and later rebuilt in 1830 by Maharaja Ranjit Singh with marble and pure gold. It surely ranks among the top India historical sites, which gives you inner peace.

8. India-gate

Description

India Gate is the most favourite historical places in India for the locals as well as for foreigners. This monument has been compared with the famous Arch of Constantine in Rome, and Arch de Triomphe in Paris. The monument was designed by Edwin Lutyens and was built in 1931 to honour the 82000 soldiers of India and Britishers who fought World War One, and also in the memory of 13300 who were martyred in the third Anglo-Afghan war.
